Walking is one of the most accessible ways to burn calories — no gym, no equipment, and easy on the joints. Whether you track a **30-minute brisk walk** or your full daily step count, the calories add up.

## Quick answer: how many calories does walking burn?

For most adults at a **moderate pace (about 3 mph)**, walking burns roughly:

- **~0.04–0.05 calories per pound of body weight per mile**
- **~100 calories per mile** for a 160–180 lb person
- **~80–170 calories in 30 minutes**, depending on pace and weight

Heavier people burn more. Brisk walking and hills burn more than slow strolling. Use our [Calories Burned Walking Calculator](/tools/walking-calorie-calculator) for a personalized number.

## Calories burned walking per km

If you measure distance in kilometers:

| Weight | Slow (~3 km/h) | Moderate (~4.8 km/h) | Brisk (~5.6 km/h) |
|--------|----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| 140 lb (64 kg) | ~55 cal/km | ~70 cal/km | ~85 cal/km |
| 160 lb (73 kg) | ~65 cal/km | ~80 cal/km | ~100 cal/km |
| 180 lb (82 kg) | ~75 cal/km | ~90 cal/km | ~110 cal/km |

**Example:** A 160 lb adult walking **4 km at moderate pace** might burn roughly **320 calories** total.

For an exact estimate, enter your stats in the [walking calorie calculator](/tools/walking-calorie-calculator).

## Calories burned walking in 30 minutes

For a **160 lb (73 kg)** adult:

| Pace | Approx. calories / 30 min | Miles in 30 min |
|------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Slow (2 mph) | 80–90 | ~1.0 |
| Moderate (3 mph) | 110–120 | ~1.5 |
| Brisk (3.5–4 mph) | 140–170 | ~1.75–2.0 |
| Very brisk (4.5+ mph) | 180+ | ~2.25+ |

**How many calories will I burn walking?** — multiply your weight in pounds by about **0.03–0.05** for a 30-minute moderate walk. The calculator above refines this using your exact pace.

## What affects calories burned

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Body weight** | Heavier people burn more per mile |
| **Pace** | Brisk walking burns significantly more than strolling |
| **Terrain** | Hills and uneven ground increase effort |
| **Duration** | Minutes matter as much as distance |

## Walking 4 miles: how many calories?

**4 miles of walking** at moderate pace might burn:

- **140 lb person:** ~360–400 calories
- **160 lb person:** ~400–450 calories
- **180 lb person:** ~450–500 calories

Faster pace or hills push these numbers higher. Pair distance goals with our [Step Goal Calculator](/tools/step-goal-calculator) to translate steps into miles.

## Walking and weight loss

Walking alone rarely creates a large deficit — but it **stacks** with nutrition:

- **500 cal/day deficit** from food → ~1 lb/week
- **+150 cal** from a 30-min brisk walk → faster progress or more food flexibility

Pair walking estimates with your [TDEE calculator](/tools/daily-calorie-calculator) and [food log](/tools/food-calorie-calculator) for a complete picture. Read [walking for weight loss — how much you need](/articles/walking-for-weight-loss-how-much) for weekly mileage targets.

## Walk at the right intensity

**Moderate intensity** means you can talk but not sing. For heart health, that often aligns with **50–70% of max heart rate**.

## Frequently asked questions

**How many calories does walking burn per mile?**  
About 0.04–0.05 calories per pound at moderate pace — roughly 80–120 calories per mile for most adults.

**Is walking enough for weight loss?**  
Walking plus a modest calorie deficit often works well. Diet drives most fat loss; walking improves adherence and heart health.

**Does walking speed matter?**  
Yes. Brisk walking (3.5–4 mph) can burn 40–50% more calories than slow strolling in the same time.

## When to talk to your doctor

Stop and seek care for chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or dizziness during walks. Ask before starting if you have heart disease, balance issues, or recent joint surgery.
