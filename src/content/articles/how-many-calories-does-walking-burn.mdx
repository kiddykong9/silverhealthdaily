Walking is one of the most underrated tools for weight management and heart health. It is free, joint-friendly, and the calories add up — especially if you walk daily.

## What affects calories burned

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Body weight** | Heavier people burn more per mile |
| **Pace** | Brisk walking burns significantly more than strolling |
| **Terrain** | Hills and uneven ground increase effort |
| **Duration** | Minutes matter as much as distance |

## Rough estimates (30 minutes)

For a **160 lb (73 kg)** adult:

| Pace | Approx. calories / 30 min |
|------|---------------------------|
| Slow (2 mph) | 80–90 |
| Moderate (3 mph) | 110–120 |
| Brisk (3.5–4 mph) | 140–170 |
| Very brisk (4.5+ mph) | 180+ |

Your numbers will differ. Use our [Walking Calorie Calculator](/tools/walking-calorie-calculator) for a personalized estimate.

## Walking and weight loss

Walking alone rarely creates a large deficit — but it **stacks** with nutrition:

- **500 cal/day deficit** from food → ~1 lb/week
- **+150 cal** from a 30-min brisk walk → faster progress or more food flexibility

Pair walking estimates with your [TDEE](/tools/daily-calorie-calculator) and [food log](/tools/food-calorie-calculator) for a complete picture.

## Steps vs. minutes

10,000 steps might equal **4–5 miles** depending on stride — roughly **300–450 calories** for many adults. Step count is a useful proxy; pace still matters.

Set a daily target with the [Step Goal Calculator](/tools/step-goal-calculator).

## Walk at the right intensity

**Moderate intensity** means you can talk but not sing. For heart health, that often aligns with **50–70% of max heart rate**.

Check your zone: [Target Heart Rate Calculator](/tools/target-heart-rate-calculator). Read more in [target heart rate when walking](/articles/target-heart-rate-when-walking).

## Build a walking habit

- Start with **10–15 minutes** after a meal
- Add a [7-Day Walking Plan](/tools/7-day-walking-plan)
- Track movement in the [Habit Tracker](/tools/habit-tracker)
- Increase **time before speed** if joints are sensitive

## When to talk to your doctor

Stop and seek care for chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or dizziness during walks. Ask before starting if you have heart disease, balance issues, or recent joint surgery.
