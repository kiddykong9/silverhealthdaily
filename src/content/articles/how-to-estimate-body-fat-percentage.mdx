## Quick answer: how to calculate body fat percentage

You can estimate body fat at home in two ways:

1. **Tape measure (US Navy method)** — measure neck, waist, and hips (women). Most accurate DIY option.
2. **BMI-based formula** — uses height, weight, age, and sex. Less accurate but needs no tape.

## Why body fat matters

Two people can share the same BMI but different body composition. Higher body fat — especially around the abdomen — is linked to greater metabolic and cardiovascular risk in many studies.

Tracking body fat over months (not days) helps you see whether weight loss is mostly fat or muscle.

## How to calculate body fat with a tape measure (Navy method)

Measure with a flexible tape, standing relaxed:

- **Neck** — narrowest point below the larynx
- **Waist** — at navel level, not sucked in
- **Hips** (women only) — widest point

Enter measurements in our [Body Fat Calculator](/tools/body-fat-calculator). Accuracy is typically within **3–4%** of professional methods for most people — good enough for trends.

### Tips for accurate measurements

- Measure at the **same time of day** (morning is common)
- Use the **same tape placement** each month
- Do not pull the tape tight — snug contact only
- Stand relaxed; do not flex or suck in your stomach

## How to count body fat percentage without a tape (BMI estimate)

If you do not have a tape measure, a formula using BMI, age, and sex gives a rough estimate. Less accurate — especially for muscular adults — but useful as a starting point. Switch to the **BMI estimate** tab in the same calculator.

## How to find out body fat percentage: professional options

| Method | Accuracy | Cost |
|--------|----------|------|
| Navy tape (home) | ±3–4% | Free |
| DEXA scan | Gold standard | $50–150 |
| Hydrostatic weighing | Very accurate | Gym/clinic |
| Bioimpedance scale | Variable | $30–100+ |

Worth professional testing if you are training seriously or your doctor recommends closer monitoring.

## General body fat reference ranges

| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Essential fat | 2–5% | 10–13% |
| Athletes | 6–13% | 14–20% |
| Fitness | 14–17% | 21–24% |
| Average | 18–24% | 25–31% |

Individual targets vary. Health is not defined by reaching "athlete" levels.

## Frequently asked questions

**How often should I measure body fat?**  
Monthly is enough for most people. Daily fluctuations reflect water, not true fat change.

**Is body fat percentage better than BMI?**  
They answer different questions. BMI is a quick screen; body fat estimates composition. Use both for context.

**Can I calculate body fat percentage from weight alone?**  
No — you need at least height and weight (BMI method) or tape measurements (Navy method).

## When to see a doctor

Very low body fat can disrupt hormones and immunity. Rapid loss without trying, or inability to gain healthy weight, also deserves medical evaluation.
