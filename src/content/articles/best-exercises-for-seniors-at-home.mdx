You don't need a gym membership to stay strong after 50. A consistent at-home routine can improve balance, bone density, heart health, and daily energy — all from your living room.

## Before you start

Clear a space free of rugs you could trip on. Wear supportive shoes or go barefoot on a non-slip surface. Stop if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or sharp joint pain. When in doubt, ask your doctor or a physical therapist for clearance.

## Warm-up (5 minutes)

- March in place for 60 seconds
- Arm circles — 10 forward, 10 backward
- Gentle torso twists — 10 each side
- Ankle rolls — 10 each foot

## The 6-move home circuit

Do each exercise for 30–45 seconds, rest 15 seconds between moves. Complete 2 rounds, 3 times per week.

### 1. Chair squats

Stand in front of a sturdy chair. Lower hips back as if sitting, lightly touch the seat, stand back up. Keep knees aligned over toes.

### 2. Wall push-ups

Hands on wall at shoulder height. Bend elbows to bring chest toward wall, push back. Easier than floor push-ups but still builds upper body.

### 3. Standing calf raises

Hold a counter for balance. Rise onto toes, lower slowly. Strengthens ankles and reduces fall risk.

### 4. Seated leg extensions

Sit tall, extend one leg straight, hold 2 seconds, lower. Alternate legs. Supports knee stability.

### 5. Heel-to-toe walk

Walk a straight line placing heel directly in front of toes. Improves balance — do near a wall for support.

### 6. Single-leg stand

Hold a counter, lift one foot slightly off the floor for 10–20 seconds. Switch legs. Progress to longer holds over weeks.

## Cool-down

Stretch hamstrings, calves, and shoulders for 30 seconds each. Hydrate — use our [Water Intake Calculator](/tools/water-intake-calculator) to estimate daily needs.
