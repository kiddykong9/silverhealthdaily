"Macros" — short for **macronutrients** — are the three calorie sources in food: **protein**, **carbohydrates**, and **fat**. Each gram provides energy:

- Protein: **4 calories per gram**
- Carbohydrates: **4 calories per gram**
- Fat: **9 calories per gram**

Tracking macros is optional, but understanding the split helps you eat enough protein, stay satisfied, and lose weight without feeling deprived.

## Step 1: Know your daily calorie target

Macros are percentages or grams of your **total daily calories**. Start with maintenance (TDEE), then subtract a modest deficit if you are trying to lose weight.

Use the [Daily Calorie Calculator](/tools/daily-calorie-calculator) for maintenance, then the [Calorie Deficit Calculator](/tools/calorie-deficit-calculator) for a safe loss target.

Example: if your loss target is **1,800 calories per day**, macros are how you divide those 1,800 calories.

## Step 2: Pick a macro split

Common approaches for gradual weight loss:

| Approach | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Best for |
|----------|---------|-------|-----|----------|
| Balanced | 30% | 40% | 30% | Most people starting out |
| Higher protein | 35% | 35% | 30% | Preserving muscle after 50 |
| Lower carb | 35% | 25% | 40% | Blood sugar sensitivity (ask your doctor) |

There is no single best split. Higher protein often helps older adults because it supports muscle and keeps you full.

Our [Macro Calculator](/tools/macro-calculator) turns your calorie target into daily grams of protein, carbs, and fat instantly.

## Step 3: Prioritize protein first

For a 160 lb (73 kg) adult, **0.8–1.2 g of protein per kg** is a reasonable daily range — roughly **60–90 grams**, depending on activity.

When cutting calories, **protect protein** rather than slashing it. Muscle loss during weight loss is common without enough protein and strength work.

Double-check with the [Protein Calculator](/tools/protein-calculator).

## Example: 1,800 calories, balanced split

At 30% protein / 40% carbs / 30% fat:

- **Protein:** 540 cal ÷ 4 = **135 g**
- **Carbs:** 720 cal ÷ 4 = **180 g**
- **Fat:** 540 cal ÷ 9 = **60 g**

That is a starting framework — adjust based on hunger, energy, and how meals feel in real life.

## Simple meals that hit macros

You do not need a food scale for every bite. Visual portions work:

- **Palm** — protein (fish, chicken, tofu, Greek yogurt)
- **Fist** — carbs (rice, potatoes, fruit, whole grains)
- **Thumb** — fats (olive oil, nuts, avocado)

Log a few typical days in the [Food Nutrition Calculator](/tools/food-calorie-calculator) to see where you land. Patterns beat perfection.

## Common mistakes

- **Cutting protein too low** — leads to hunger and muscle loss
- **Ignoring fiber** — carbs from vegetables and whole grains digest slower
- **All-or-nothing thinking** — one high-calorie meal does not ruin a week
- **Skipping strength work** — walking helps, but resistance training protects muscle during a deficit

## When to talk to your doctor

Consult your care team before a structured weight-loss plan if you take insulin or diabetes medications, have kidney disease, or have had disordered eating. Macro targets should fit your health profile — not a generic template.
