Blood pressure measures the force of blood against artery walls. It rises and falls throughout the day — but consistently high readings strain the heart, kidneys, and brain over time.

## What is normal blood pressure?

For most adults, **normal is below 120/80 mmHg**:

| Category | Systolic (top) | Diastolic (bottom) |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Normal | Less than 120 | Less than 80 |
| Elevated | 120–129 | Less than 80 |
| High (Stage 1) | 130–139 | 80–89 |
| High (Stage 2) | 140+ | 90+ |

One high reading is not a diagnosis. Doctors look at averages over multiple visits and home readings.

## Does blood pressure change with age?

Arteries stiffen with age, so systolic pressure often creeps up after 50. That does **not** mean high numbers are harmless — tighter control still reduces stroke and heart attack risk.

Work with your doctor on a personal target. Many adults over 60 aim for **less than 130/80** if tolerated, but individual goals vary.

## When to call your doctor

Seek care for readings **above 180/120**, chest pain, severe headache, vision changes, or shortness of breath. For routine highs, book a visit — don't rely on online charts alone.
