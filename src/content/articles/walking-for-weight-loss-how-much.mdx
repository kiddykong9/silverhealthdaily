Walking is one of the most underrated weight-loss tools — especially after 50. It's sustainable, low-impact, and easy to repeat daily. But walking alone rarely outruns a high-calorie diet. **Diet + walking** is the winning pair.

## How many calories does walking burn?

Rough rule: **~100 calories per mile** for a 150-lb adult at moderate pace. Heavier people burn more; faster pace burns more.

Get a personalized number with our [Walking Calorie Calculator](/tools/walking-calorie-calculator).

## How much walking for weight loss?

To lose **1 lb of fat**, you need roughly a **3,500 calorie deficit** total. Walking alone:

| Daily walk | Approx. weekly burn (150 lb) | Fat loss if diet unchanged |
|------------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| 30 min (~1.5 mi) | ~1,050 cal | ~0.3 lb/week |
| 60 min (~3 mi) | ~2,100 cal | ~0.6 lb/week |

Real-world loss is usually **higher** when you also eat in a modest deficit. Walking makes sticking to diet easier — it improves mood, sleep, and insulin sensitivity.

## Steps vs. minutes

**7,000–10,000 steps/day** is a solid target for most sedentary-to-moderate adults. Use the [Step Goal Calculator](/tools/step-goal-calculator) for a personalized number.

Steps from chores, errands, and work count — you don't need a treadmill.

## Common mistakes

- **Reward eating** — "I walked so I earned dessert" often cancels the burn
- **Only weekend long walks** — daily consistency beats one big hike
- **Ignoring pace** — leisurely strolling burns less; aim for "slightly breathless"
